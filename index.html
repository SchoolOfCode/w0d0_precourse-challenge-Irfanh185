Sir Alexander Chapman Ferguson CBE (born 31 December 1941) is a Scottish former football manager and player, known for managing Manchester United from 1986 to 2013. He is widely regarded as the greatest football manager of all time,and has won more trophies than any other manager in the history of football.

Ferguson played as a forward for several Scottish clubs, including Dunfermline Athletic and Rangers. Ferguson then enjoyed a highly successful period as manager of Aberdeen, winning three Scottish league championships, four Scottish Cups and the UEFA Cup Winners' Cup in 1983. He briefly managed Scotland following the death of Jock Stein, taking the team to the 1986 World Cup.

Ferguson was appointed manager of Manchester United in November 1986.
